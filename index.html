François d’Assise Sanou

Wireless Networks Researcher

I am a computer scientist and PhD student at the Department of Computer Science, Nazi Bony University in Burkina Faso. My research focuses on simulation, optimisation and analysis of Long‑Range (LoRa) and LoRaWAN networks, with a broader interest in low‑power IoT systems.

Currently I lead the development of LoRaFlexSim, a modular simulator that reproduces the main behaviours of LoRaWAN while enabling new research avenues.

About

I am passionate about wireless communications and the Internet of Things. Through my doctoral work I design and evaluate novel mechanisms for long‑range low‑power networks. Outside academia I enjoy sharing knowledge through teaching and collaborating with fellow researchers on open‑source simulation tools.

Research Interests

  • Performance evaluation and optimisation of LoRaWAN and LPWAN networks
  • Adaptive data rate algorithms and intelligent resource management
  • Energy‑efficient communication protocols for IoT devices
  • Simulation frameworks and reproducible research

Experience

My professional journey revolves around research and teaching in wireless networks. Below is a summary of my recent roles.

Projects

My work spans the development of simulation frameworks and research into new mechanisms for low‑power wide‑area networks.

  • LoRaFlexSim – a modular, event‑driven simulator for LoRa and LoRaWAN networks. The project focuses on fidelity, extensibility and ease of use. I lead the design and implementation of this tool. Visit the project website.
  • Implementing LoRaWAN End‑Device Classes A, B, and C in LoRaFlexSim – a project that extends LoRaFlexSim to fully support all LoRaWAN device classes and explores their performance in various scenarios. Read the paper.
  • Adaptive Algorithms for LPWANs – ongoing research into intelligent resource management and adaptive data rate schemes to improve network efficiency and device lifetime.

Publications

Below is an updated list of my publications. The first paper explores modulation adaptation using the FLoRa simulator; the others employ LoRaFlexSim.

  1. Dynamic Adaptation of the Modulation in a LoRa Channel According to the Link Quality – proposes adaptive modulation techniques based on link quality measurements. View on IEEE Xplore.
  2. LoRaFlexSim: A Flexible LoRa/LoRaWAN Simulator – presents the architecture, validation and capabilities of LoRaFlexSim. View on IEEE Xplore.
  3. Implementing LoRaWAN End‑Device Classes A, B, and C in LoRaFlexSim – details the implementation and evaluation of LoRaWAN device classes using LoRaFlexSim. View on IEEE Xplore.
  4. Quantifying Mobility Range Interactions in LoRaWAN with LoRaFlexSim – quantifies mobility range effects in LoRaWAN networks using LoRaFlexSim. View on IEEE Xplore.
