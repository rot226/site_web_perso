François d’Assise Sanou

Wireless Networks Researcher

I am a computer scientist and PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Université Nazi Bony, Burkina Faso. I investigate LoRa and LoRaWAN systems through simulation, optimization, and performance analysis.

I develop LoRaFlexSim, a modular simulator that reproduces key LoRaWAN behaviors and supports reproducible research workflows.

About

I focus on wireless communications and the Internet of Things. In my doctoral work, I develop and evaluate mechanisms for long-range, low-power networks. I also contribute to teaching and collaborative open-source simulation tools.

Research Interests

  • Performance evaluation and optimization of LoRaWAN and LPWAN networks.
  • Adaptive data rate algorithms and intelligent resource allocation.
  • Energy-efficient communication protocols for connected devices.
  • Simulation frameworks and reproducible research methods.

Experience

My academic path combines research and teaching in wireless networks.

  • PhD Candidate, Department of Computer Science, Université Nazi Bony (2025 - present): investigate LoRaWAN simulation frameworks, develop adaptive algorithms, and evaluate network performance.
  • Research Assistant, Université Nazi Bony (2023 - 2025): developed and validated LoRaWAN simulation models and contributed to open-source tools.
  • Teaching Assistant, Université Nazi Bony (2023 - 2024): supported courses in wireless networks and IoT, and supervised undergraduate students.

Projects

My projects investigate simulation methods and develop mechanisms for low-power wide-area networks.

  • LoRaFlexSim: modular event-driven simulator for LoRa and LoRaWAN networks. I lead architecture design and implementation. Project page.
  • Implementing LoRaWAN End-Device Classes A, B, and C in LoRaFlexSim: extension of LoRaFlexSim to support all LoRaWAN end-device classes and evaluate behavior across scenarios. Read paper.
  • Adaptive Algorithms for LPWAN: ongoing work to develop resource-aware data rate mechanisms and evaluate gains in network efficiency and device lifetime.

Publications

Selected publications on LoRa and LoRaWAN network modeling and analysis.

  1. Dynamic Adaptation of the Modulation in a LoRa Channel According to the Link Quality: investigates modulation adaptation strategies based on link quality indicators. Read paper.
  2. LoRaFlexSim: A Flexible LoRa/LoRaWAN Simulator: presents architecture, validation, and extensibility of LoRaFlexSim. Read paper.
  3. Implementing LoRaWAN End-Device Classes A, B, and C in LoRaFlexSim: develops and evaluates end-device class implementations using LoRaFlexSim. Read paper.
  4. Quantifying Mobility Range Interactions in LoRaWAN with LoRaFlexSim: evaluates mobility effects in LoRaWAN scenarios with a simulation-based approach. Read paper.
